Research Vision

My research programme aims to establish Strong AI through biologically inspired architectures and to enable controlled, predictable biology via digital infrastructures. I envision a closed‑loop paradigm where distributed biosensor networks feed real‑time data into neuro‑symbolic AI systems, which in turn model and control biological processes – from plant stress responses to animal behaviour. I propose to create a distributed sensor‑based digital infrastructure for biological system modelling, integrating relational representations, mnemonic interfaces, and digital organism concepts to transform biology from a descriptive science into an engineering discipline.

This vision was ahead of its time. When I first proposed the integration of biosensors, wireless networks, and AI for biological system modelling in the late 2000s, the idea was met with skepticism — even laughter — among my peers in cybernetics. Today, these concepts are recognized as essential for precision agriculture, environmental monitoring, and digital biology. My early work on portable fluorimetry (2006–2008) and distributed sensor networks (2013–2016) laid the foundation for a research direction that has been developing at the V.M. Glushkov Institute of Cybernetics for almost two decades. This experience taught me that transformative science requires the courage to pursue ideas that others do not yet understand — and that is exactly the spirit I bring to my ERC Advanced Grant proposal.

From knowledge networks to cognitive AI. My 2014 patent on a global network for automatic knowledge taking (UA 89025) was the first step toward what I now develop as cognitive AI architectures. The idea of creating systems that can automatically acquire, structure, and reason with knowledge has evolved into my current work on neuro-symbolic AI, mnemonic interfaces, and relational representations — published in my recent papers on Cognitive Interaction Layers (2025) and Abstract Biology (2025). This continuity of thought, from a patent to cutting-edge AI research, reflects my long-term commitment to building intelligent systems that truly understand and model complex biological and cognitive processes.
